## The Core Pattern: Skills + Prompts

The most effective marketing automation follows a simple pattern:

1. **Build a skill** for a specific task (e.g., "Facebook Ad Generator", "SEO Analyzer")
2. **Wire it to a trigger** (Telegram button, scheduled cron, Discord slash command)
3. **Let it run** — No manual intervention needed

### Example: Build a Social Media Manager Skill

**Goal:** Your OpenClaw manages all your social media replies and DMs.

**Steps:**
1. Create a skill that:
   - Fetches your latest tweets/posts
   - Identifies high-engagement comments
   - Drafts thoughtful replies in your voice
   - Posts via Typefully (for review) or directly (if approved)

2. Schedule it to run every 4 hours via cron

3. Customize your SOUL.md so the agent knows your voice and values

**Prompt template:**
```
You are a social media manager for [name/brand]. Your job:
1. Check [platform] for recent posts with 10+ replies
2. Identify the highest-quality, most thoughtful replies
3. Draft 2-3 response options in this voice: [your voice guide]
4. For each reply, assign a priority (respond now / respond later / skip)

Format output as:
- Post: [link]
- Top reply: [quote]
- My response: [your response]
- Priority: [now/later/skip]

Constraints:
- Replies must be genuine, not marketing-y
- Never argue or get defensive
- Aim to deepen conversations, not sell
```

## Use Cases & Implementation

### 1. SEO Automation (Zero Cost, Agency-Level)

**What it does:** Analyzes your competitors, identifies SEO opportunities, generates keyword-targeted content.

**Skills you need:**
- Content research (via web search + firecrawl)
- Keyword analysis (via Serpstat API if available, or manual analysis)
- Content generation (built-in)

**Implementation prompt:**
```
You are an SEO strategist. For the topic [topic], do:
1. Research top-ranking competitors (sites currently ranking #1-10)
2. Extract their keywords, backlink strategies, content structure
3. Identify gaps — keywords they're missing or underexploring
4. Suggest 5 content pieces we should create to own this niche
5. For each piece, provide: keyword target, search intent, outline, estimated traffic

Use firecrawl to scrape top pages for structure analysis.
```

**Output:** A weekly SEO briefing with actionable content ideas.

**What it does:** Generates platform-specific content (tweets, threads, TikTok scripts, LinkedIn posts, blog articles).

**Skills you need:**
- x-research (for trending topics)
- voice-matcher (your SOUL.md)
- platform-formatter (tweet ≠ LinkedIn ≠ TikTok)

```
4. 1 TikTok script (30-60 seconds)

For each:
- Research trending topics in [niche] from last 24h
- Generate content that's authentic to [name]'s perspective
- Include hooks, structure (intro/body/CTA), and talking points
- Tag relevant accounts/hashtags

Format:
## [Platform]

[content]

**Hook:** [why people should care]
**Talking points:** [main ideas]
**CTA:** [call to action]
```

### 3. Ads & Growth (Rapid Testing)

**What it does:** Generates 50+ ad variations in minutes, tests copy frameworks, identifies what works.

**Skills you need:**
- Copywriting templates (hooks, pain points, benefits)
- A/B test runner (if you have ad account API access)

**Implementation prompt:**
```
You are a performance marketer. Generate 20 Facebook ad variations for [product/service].

Use these frameworks:
- Problem-Agitate-Solve (PAS)
- Comparison (ours vs status quo)
- Curiosity + benefit
- Social proof + urgency
- Value prop + objection handling

For each, provide:
- Headline (max 27 chars)
- Primary text (max 125 chars)
- Description (max 30 chars)
- Estimated CTR (based on copy strength)

Organize by framework so we can A/B test frameworks too.
```

### 4. TikTok Slideshow Growth Engine

**What it does:** Automates viral TikTok slideshow creation and posting. Slideshows get disproportionate reach for the effort required and can be fully automated. (Credit: @wickedguro)

**Setup:**
1. VPN with dedicated US mobile IP (NordVPN, ExpressVPN — mobile IPs, not datacenter)
2. Keep VPN connected. Set phone to US locale. Remove GPS tracking.
3. Register TikTok in US locale. Engage with niche content first to train audience targeting.
4. Install [Postiz](https://postiz.com) for scheduling across TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube from one CLI

**Automation prompt:**
```
Build a TikTok growth skill. Every day:
1. Research trending topics in [niche] from the last 24h
2. Generate a 5-8 slide slideshow on the top topic
3. Use [your content style] for slide copy
4. Export slides as images to workspace/content/tiktok/
5. Schedule via Postiz CLI for [optimal time]
6. Log to workspace/content/published/tiktok-[date].md
```

Scale: once a format works, post the same slideshow across multiple accounts via Postiz.

> **Mobile-first:** The OpenClaw Android app makes it easy to review and approve TikTok content on the go. Get a Telegram notification, preview the slides on your phone, approve with one tap.

### 5. Community & Support (Scaling Conversations)

**What it does:** Manages comments, DMs, support tickets at scale without sounding robotic.

**Skills you need:**
- Sentiment analysis (is this comment positive/negative/question?)
- Response prioritization (urgent vs nice-to-have)
- Multi-tone voice (sales vs support vs education)

**Implementation prompt:**
```
You manage community for [platform/brand]. Your role:
1. Review unread comments/DMs
2. Categorize by intent: question / feedback / complaint / praise / spam
3. For each category, draft an appropriate response
4. Prioritize urgent (> 100 replies, negative sentiment, creator asking question)

Tone guidelines:
- Questions: Helpful, thorough, educational
- Complaints: Empathetic, problem-solving, transparent
- Praise: Genuine, specific, inviting deeper connection
- Feedback: Thoughtful, non-defensive, action-oriented

Format:
[Message]
Category: [type]
Priority: [high/medium/low]
Draft response: [response]
```

## Selling Skills as Products

The skills marketplace model: don't sell tools, sell results. (Credit: @wickedguro)

- ❌ "Postiz scheduling skill" — nobody buys tools

Package your best workflows as skills with a specific result in the title. Add affiliate links to the tools used in the skill — even free skills can generate revenue.

## CLI-First Tool Design

If you're building tools your agent will use, design them CLI-first. (Credit: @wickedguro)

Why CLI > API for agents:
- Less code for the agent to write → fewer tokens
- Less context rot from complex API wrappers
- Fewer mistakes — CLIs are predictable

Tools the community has built or is building:
- `postiz-cli` — social media scheduling
- `agent-browser` — browser automation
- `beeper-cli` — messaging inbox access
- `x-research` — Twitter/X research

When evaluating an API integration, ask: is there a CLI alternative? If not, build one. Your future agent sessions will thank you.

## Advanced: Multi-Agent Orchestration

### Agent Teams for Collaborative Marketing

Claude Code's agent teams feature lets multiple AI agents coordinate simultaneously, each with specialized roles. This is where marketing automation scales from good to exceptional.

**Use case: Campaign Research Sprint**
```
Create an agent team to research launch strategy for [product]:
- Agent 1: Competitor ad analysis (dissects competitor creatives, messaging, targeting)
- Agent 2: Customer voice mining (analyzes reviews, Reddit, support tickets for real objections)
- Agent 3: Positioning pressure test (challenges assumptions with devil's advocate role)

Requirement: They must debate findings and reach consensus before synthesizing into a strategy doc.
```

**Real results:** 1 agent exploring alone anchors on the first decent idea. 3 agents actively debating? The angle that survives is battle-tested.

**Use case: Parallel Content Production**
```
Create a team for this week's content pipeline:
- Agent 1: Research (competitive gaps, trending topics, SEO opportunities)
- Agent 2: Writing (article drafting based on Agent 1 research)
- Agent 3: QA (plagiarism check, readability, accuracy verification, fact-checking)

Setting: Research and production happen simultaneously instead of sequentially.
```

**Setup:**
1. Enable in settings.json: `"CLAUDE_CODE_EXPERIMENTAL_AGENT_TEAMS": "1"`
2. Use delegate mode (Shift+Tab) so the lead only coordinates, not executes
3. Require plan approval for high-stakes work
4. Use shared task list (Ctrl+T) to monitor progress

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- Don't use teams for simple tasks (overhead isn't worth it)
- Don't let agents edit the same files (coordinated collisions)
- 3-5 agents is optimal (more = coordination overhead)
- Always require plan approval on complex work

**Cost consideration:** Token usage scales with team size. Each agent is a separate Claude instance. Worth it for complex research/production, overkill for one-off copy tweaks.

### Content Pipeline at Scale (The Morgan Pattern)

Real case study: One founder built a system that published 80+ articles in 10 days with a squad of OpenClaw agents.

**The Architecture:**
- 🔍 **Scout** — Finds topics with keyword research (runs every 6h)
- ✍️ **Quill** — Writes 2000+ word SEO articles (runs hourly)
- 📚 **Sage** — Reviews for quality/plagiarism/readability (3x/day)
- 📢 **Ezra** — Publishes to production (every 3h)
- 📣 **Herald** — Amplifies on social media (2x/day)
- 👀 **Lurker** — Scouts Reddit for outreach opportunities (8h)
- 📊 **Archie** — Weekly analytics reports
- 📋 **Morgan** — Project manager (3x/day) — Unblocks bottlenecks

**The Pipeline:**
```
Scout → Quill → Sage → Ezra → Herald → Archie
         ↓
    Reject? Send back to Quill for revision
```

All agents coordinate through a shared Notion database with atomic status transitions (Backlog → To Do → In Progress → Review → Ready to Publish → Done).

**Key Implementation Details:**

*Specialization over generalization:* Each agent is great at ONE thing instead of okay at everything.
- Scout has keyword research tools + competitor analysis
- Quill has brand voice guidelines + SEO rules
- Sage has plagiarism checker (Copyscape), readability scoring (Flesch-Kincaid), accuracy verification

*Quality gates that actually reject:* 40% of first drafts get rejected by Sage with specific revision notes. After revision, 95%+ pass.
- Plagiarism via Copyscape (~$0.03 per check)
- SEO scoring on 100-point rubric
- Readability must score 60+
- Factual accuracy check

*Claim locking for parallel agents:* When multiple agents query for work simultaneously:
1. Each agent generates unique claim ID
2. Immediately writes claim + status change atomically
3. Re-fetches to verify claim stuck
4. If claim fails, tries next article
5. Add 25-second stagger between parallel agents

Result: Zero collisions.

**Real Results (10 Days):**
- 80+ articles written
- 41 published
- ~40% first-draft rejection rate
- $0.70 cost per article
- 15 minutes human time per day
- 15,000+ Google impressions generated
- 130+ clicks from search traffic

### The Marketing Supercomputer

Build expert advisors trained on the greatest marketing minds in history. This transforms generic AI output into specialized strategic guidance.

**The System (3 Layers):**

1. **Access Knowledge** — Scrape YouTube transcripts from top marketers (Alex Hormozi, Seth Godin, Alex Hartley, etc.)
2. **Extract Frameworks** — Have Claude identify each unique principle, framework, and mental model
3. **Build Advisors** — Create reusable instruction sets that channel each expert's thinking

**Step 1: Gather Content**
```
"Go to [YouTube channel]. Scrape every transcript. Also search for '[person name] interview'
and '[person name] podcast' and pull top 50 results. Save as separate text files."

Result: 200+ pages of transcripts in <1 hour
```

**Step 2: Build Folder Structure**
```
marketing-supercomputer/
├── hormozi/
├── godin/
├── hartley/
├── scripts/
├── output/
└── CLAUDE.md
```

**Step 3: Create CLAUDE.md (Most Important)**
```
I am [your name], founder of [your company]. I sell [your offer] to [your audience].

This folder contains transcripts from Alex Hormozi's videos, podcasts, and guest appearances.

Your job is to EXTRACT, not summarize.

For every distinct framework or repeatable principle:
- Name it using Hormozi's own terminology
- State the core principle in one sentence
- Include a direct quote from the transcript
- Explain the underlying psychology of why it works
- Show specifically how I can apply this to [your specific business context]
```

**Step 4: Extract Frameworks**
Upload transcripts as Project Knowledge. Run:
```
"Read every transcript. Extract every distinct framework, methodology, mental model,
and repeatable principle. If frameworks connect, note the relationships."
```

**Step 5: Build the Advisor**
```
You are a strategic advisor trained on Alex Hormozi's complete body of work. When I ask
a question in your area of expertise, apply the specific frameworks and principles extracted
from their content. Score my work 1-10 on each dimension. Identify the weakest element and
explain using their reasoning. Every recommendation must be grounded in something they've
actually taught. Push back if I'm making a mistake they've explicitly warned against.
```

**Real Example:** Landing page scored 6/10 by the Miller advisor. Issue: Leading with product features instead of customer problem. Villain buried too deep. Rewrote based on advisor feedback → new page scored 9/10.

**Cost:** One-time setup. Zero ongoing cost. Access to centuries of collected business wisdom.

## Common Pitfalls

### 1. **Robotic Voice**
If your content sounds like AI wrote it, check your SOUL.md. Include:
- Your actual writing samples (3–5 tweets/posts you've written)
- Your personality quirks (do you use em dashes? curse? make jokes?)
- Your perspective (what do you believe, strongly?)

### 2. **Generic Prompts**
The difference between mediocre and great marketing automation is specificity:
- ❌ "Write a tweet about marketing"
- ✅ "You're @jackfriks, a marketing automation expert. You believe most agencies are overpriced. Your audience is indie hackers and solopreneurs. Write a tweet about why they should build their own marketing stack instead of hiring an agency. Include a spicy take and a link. Use your voice: sarcastic but helpful."

### 3. **Not Measuring**
Track everything:
- Engagement rate (likes/views)
- Reply rate (how many replies per post)
- Link clicks (did it drive traffic?)
- Conversion rate (from traffic to signup/sale)

Use these metrics to refine prompts weekly.

### 4. **Forgetting the Human Touch**
Automation is best for volume, not nuance. Reserve human time for:
- High-value conversations (CEOs, press, major customers)
- Crisis communication (when something breaks)
- Strategic decisions (quarterly planning)
